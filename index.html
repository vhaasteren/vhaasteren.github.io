I am a NASA Einstein postdoctoral fellow in astrophysics, working on various data analysis topics related to pulsar timing and gravitational-wave detection in general. I am based at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory and the California Institute of Technology as part of the TAPIR group. I am a member of the North American Nanohertz Observatory for Gravitational Waves (NANOGrav) and the International Pulsar Timing Array (IPTA).

I am interested in various topics in data analysis, with my most active area of research is related to pulsar timing and gravitational-wave detection. This includes noise modeling and mitigation, time series analysis, Bayesian methods, Markov Chain Monte Carlo techniques, algorithmic design, testing general relativity, and much more. See my Research page for more details about my research.

Before becoming an Einstein fellow, I completed my masters degree in theoretical physics at Leiden University, the Netherlands. In continued my PhD in astrophysics in Leiden under the supervision of Yuri Levin. After that, I spent two years at the Albert Einstein Institute in Hannover, Germany.
